## Procedure

### Step 1: Reagent Preparation (In Glovebox, 1 hour)

1. ☐ Transfer all reagents into glovebox antechamber
2. ☐ Evacuate/refill antechamber 3× with Ar
3. ☐ Weigh reagents inside glovebox:

   | Reagent | Target (g) | Actual (g) | ✓ |
   |---------|------------|------------|---|
   | InCl₃ | 2.212 | _______ | ☐ |
   | LiCl | 0.763 | _______ | ☐ |
   | LiBr | 1.042 | _______ | ☐ |

4. ☐ Combine all reagents in agate mortar
5. ☐ Hand-grind for **15 minutes** until homogeneous fine powder

   > ⚠️ **CRITICAL:** Intimate mixing is essential for phase-pure product. Powder should appear uniform with no visible crystals.

6. ☐ Transfer to ball mill jar OR continue hand grinding

---

### Step 2: Mechanochemical Synthesis (Ball Milling, 4-8 hours)

**Option A: Ball Mill (Preferred)**

1. ☐ Load mixed powder into ZrO₂ ball mill jar (inside glovebox)
2. ☐ Add ZrO₂ balls: 10 × 10mm diameter (ball:powder ≈ 30:1 by mass)
3. ☐ Seal jar with Ar atmosphere
4. ☐ Remove from glovebox
5. ☐ Ball mill parameters:
   - Speed: **400 rpm**
   - Time: **4 hours** (8 × 30 min with 5 min rest)
   - Direction: Alternating

   > Ball milling drives the solid-state reaction and reduces particle size.

6. ☐ Return jar to glovebox
7. ☐ Collect powder, scrape walls carefully

**Option B: Hand Grinding + Thermal Treatment**

1. ☐ Continue hand grinding for **30 minutes total**
2. ☐ Proceed directly to Step 3 (thermal treatment)

**Observation after milling:**
- Color: _______ (expect: white to light gray)
- Texture: _______ (expect: fine powder)

---

### Step 3: Thermal Annealing (6 hours)

**Reaction (solid-state):**
```
18 LiCl + 12 LiBr + 10 InCl₃ → 10 Li₃InCl₄.₈Br₁.₂
```

1. ☐ Press powder into pellets (13 mm × ~2 mm) at **5 ton** pressure
2. ☐ Place pellets in alumina crucible
3. ☐ Load crucible into quartz tube
4. ☐ Seal tube with Ar flow (50 mL/min) OR vacuum-seal in quartz ampoule
5. ☐ Insert into tube furnace
6. ☐ Temperature program:

   | Step | Temp | Rate | Hold |
   |------|------|------|------|
   | Ramp 1 | RT → 200°C | 2°C/min | 1 hour |
   | Ramp 2 | 200°C → 450°C | 2°C/min | - |
   | Anneal | 450°C | - | **4 hours** |
   | Cool | 450°C → RT | 2°C/min | - |

   > ⚠️ **CRITICAL:** Do NOT exceed 500°C. Decomposition begins above 520°C.

7. ☐ Allow to cool under Ar atmosphere
8. ☐ Transfer sealed tube back to glovebox

**Observations:**
- Pellet color after annealing: _______ (expect: white, slight yellow OK)
- Pellet integrity: _______ (expect: solid, may have slight shrinkage)

---

### Step 4: Post-Processing (In Glovebox, 1 hour)

1. ☐ Remove pellets from crucible
2. ☐ Break pellets and grind in agate mortar (**10 minutes**)
3. ☐ If second annealing desired for higher purity:
   - Re-pelletize
   - Repeat Step 3 at 400°C for 2 hours
4. ☐ Final grinding to fine powder
5. ☐ Store in sealed vial under Ar

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Gray/black color | Decomposition or contamination | Use fresh reagents, lower anneal temp |
| Low conductivity | Incomplete reaction | Increase ball mill time, re-anneal |
| Secondary XRD peaks | Phase separation | Improve mixing, adjust stoichiometry |
| Pellet crumbles | Insufficient sintering | Press harder, anneal longer |
| Hygroscopic absorption | Moisture exposure | Re-dry at 200°C under Ar, improve handling |
| Poor cycling | Interfacial resistance | Add 1 wt% LiF coating on cathode |
