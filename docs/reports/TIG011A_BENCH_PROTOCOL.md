## Procedure

### Step 1: Setup (15 min)

1. ☐ Set up oil bath on hot plate with stir function
2. ☐ Add 50 mL RBF with magnetic stir bar
3. ☐ Attach reflux condenser
4. ☐ Connect nitrogen line (optional but recommended)

### Step 2: Charge Reagents (10 min)

1. ☐ Weigh **4-chloro-7-methoxyquinazoline** (500 mg, 2.57 mmol) into RBF
2. ☐ Add **n-butanol** (10 mL)
3. ☐ Stir until dissolved (~2 min)
4. ☐ Add **K₂CO₃** (710 mg, 5.14 mmol, 2.0 eq)
5. ☐ Add **N-methylpiperazine** (0.57 mL, 5.14 mmol, 2.0 eq)

**Note:** Solution should turn slightly yellow

### Step 3: Reaction (8 hours)

1. ☐ Heat oil bath to **110°C**
2. ☐ Start timer when internal temp reaches 105°C
3. ☐ Stir at 400 rpm under reflux
4. ☐ Monitor by TLC every 2 hours

**TLC Conditions:**
- Eluent: **EtOAc/MeOH/TEA (89:10:1)** ← Prevents streaking from basic piperazine
- Detection: UV 254 nm
- Rf (starting material): ~0.7
- Rf (product): ~0.3

> ⚠️ **Chemist's Edge #1:** Basic amines streak on silica. The 1% TEA neutralizes acidic silanols → crisp spots.

| Time | TLC Result | Notes |
|------|------------|-------|
| 0h | SM only | ☐ |
| 2h | SM + product | ☐ |
| 4h | Mostly product | ☐ |
| 6h | Trace SM | ☐ |
| 8h | Complete | ☐ |

### Step 4: Workup (45 min)

1. ☐ Remove from heat, cool to RT (~30 min)
2. ☐ Pour reaction mixture into **ice water** (50 mL)
3. ☐ Transfer to separatory funnel
4. ☐ **CHECK pH of aqueous layer** → Must be >10
   - If pH < 10: Add 1N NaOH dropwise until basic
   - This forces free-base form into organic layer
5. ☐ Extract with **EtOAc** (3 × 30 mL)
6. ☐ Combine organic layers
7. ☐ Wash with **brine** (2 × 25 mL)
8. ☐ Dry over **Na₂SO₄** (15 min)
9. ☐ Filter and rinse with EtOAc (10 mL)
10. ☐ Concentrate on rotovap (40°C bath, 150 mbar → 50 mbar)

> ⚠️ **Chemist's Edge #2:** TIG-011a has 2 basic N's. Acidic/neutral water protonates → product trapped in aqueous. Basify to pH >10 to recover 100%.

### Step 5: Purification (1 hour)

> ⚠️ **Chemist's Edge #3:** If crude is >85% and pale yellow, try recrystallization first (saves 2 hours).

**Option A: Recrystallization (Preferred if crude is clean)**

1. ☐ Dissolve crude in minimal **hot isopropanol** (IPA)
2. ☐ Heat to boiling, filter if any insolubles
3. ☐ Let cool slowly to RT, then to 4°C (fridge)
4. ☐ If crystals form → filter, wash with cold IPA, dry under vacuum
5. ☐ If no crystals → proceed to Option B

**Option B: Column Chromatography**

1. ☐ Pack column with silica gel (30 g) in EtOAc + 1% TEA
2. ☐ Load crude product dissolved in minimal EtOAc
3. ☐ Elute with gradient:
   - EtOAc + 1% TEA (50 mL) — discard
   - **EtOAc/MeOH/TEA 89:10:1** (100 mL) — collect fractions
   - EtOAc/MeOH/TEA 85:14:1 (50 mL) — collect fractions

4. ☐ Check fractions by TLC
5. ☐ Pool pure fractions (Rf ~0.3 in 9:1 EtOAc/MeOH)
6. ☐ Concentrate on rotovap
7. ☐ Dry under high vacuum (1 hour)

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Cause | Solution |
|---------|-------|----------|
| Reaction incomplete at 8h | Low temp | Increase to 120°C, extend 4h |
| Low yield (<50%) | Product in aqueous | Back-extract with EtOAc |
| Product not eluting | Too polar | Increase MeOH to 15% |
| Multiple spots on TLC | Impurities | Re-column or recrystallize |
| Oil instead of solid | Solvent trapped | High vac overnight |
